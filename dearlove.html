Dear Love ❤︎⁠

**.**.2029 (Tuesday)

At this point, I assume you have read all the letters I've written. Perhaps you might also have read my book. I hope you won't get annoyed by this. There are things I have always wanted to tell you all these years. By the time you're reading this, I probably won't be here anymore. This might be the last message I can send you. I've already mentioned this in my book.

I always hoped that I would never have to write this final letter, because I knew it would hurt me so much. I thought I would have passed this, but always unlucky me—I couldn't make it. How about you? Have there been any changes after all these years? How's your life going? Do you still love tulips? How's your university? All these years, I sometimes broke down so badly over one thought. You know? I used to dream that we would go to the same university since we were both in the same class. That dream lasted quite long. I never gave up on you; I used to put all my effort into you, despite the fact that we had just a tiny window to communicate. I blamed myself for a long period, thinking I shouldn't have confessed to you in the first place. If I hadn't, you wouldn't have transferred because of me. We'd have been friends all these years. That was the first stupid decision I ever made. But somehow, that decision pushed me very close to you, and we ended up building a world of memories that is still alive inside me. I used to dream that after we graduated Grade 11, we'd be in the same class the next year, and after Grade 12, we would go to the same university to make countless memories together. I knew the chances of being in the same class after Grade 11 were low. Going to the same university had an even lower chance than that. But all I dreamed of throughout that year was what I just mentioned. It sounded impossible for me at first, but I never gave up that dream until May 17, 2024. I wish I had had more chances to communicate with you throughout the whole year. I wish I could have helped you a little bit more. From the start of the year, I wish I had stayed out of trouble so that I wouldn't have become a hated person. Maybe that's the reason why you pushed me away every time we encountered each other in class. That wasn't your fault at all. Who would want to be side-by-side with such a difficult person like me? Even though I started changing myself into the person you wanted by late December, I was too late for everything. I wish I could have done something that would make you proud to call me a friend.

I once told you that I barely had any friends. You were the only friend, the only partner, who gave true meaning to my life. I know my words were immature back then. The truth was, I didn't know how to talk or communicate properly, and I didn't have the courage to admit that. Even when I was being so immature, you understood me. You never blamed me for that; you guided me to where I needed to be. You gave me a year filled with colorful vibes and joys that I didn't deserve. After that, I never saw those joys in my life again. Even if the love you gave me wasn't true, I never regretted being with you, even for a short time. I will always appreciate you for showing up in my life. As an honor, I created this website just for you. You are the very first person to visit this webpage since you scanned the QR code I sent.
